Understanding ECG Anomalies

Learn about the cardiac cycle and common irregularities.

The P-Wave

The P-wave, the first positive deflection on the ECG, represents atrial depolarization (contraction). It is essential for assessing proper electrical impulse generation from the SA node.

QRS Complex

The QRS complex corresponds to the depolarization of the ventricles. Its shape and duration are critical indicators of ventricular health and conduction system integrity.

The T-Wave

The T-wave follows the QRS complex and represents ventricular repolarization (recovery). Abnormalities here can indicate ischemia or electrolyte imbalances.
